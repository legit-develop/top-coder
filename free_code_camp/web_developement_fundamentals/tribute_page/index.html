Clive Barker

African Cup of Nations winning coach - 1996

Background

Clive William Barker (23 June 1944 – 10 June 2023) was a South African football coach. He guided the South Africa national team to their only African Nations Cup title in 1996

Barker was born in Durban, KwaZulu-Natal. He became a professional footballer in the 1960s, playing for Durban City and Durban United having made his debut at the age of 17. He had a trial with Leicester City, but a serious knee injury quickly ended his career.

Coaching Career

In a coaching career that spanned 42 years, Barker mentored teams including AmaZulu, Manning Rangers, Durban City, Durban Bush Bucks, Santos Cape Town, Wits and, as his last assignment, Maritzburg United, which came to an end in 2016.

Barker led Durban City to two back-to-back league titles in 1982 and 1983 and the Durban Bush Bucks to the honours in 1985.

"The Dog", as the coach is affectionately known in the fuootball circles will be remember for his role in helping the South African National Soccer team to win their first intercontinental cup in 1996, and his signature flying celebration whenever his team score a goal

African Cup of Nations

South Africa didn’t debut in the African continental championships until 1996. With apartheid repealed by the early 1990s, the international bans on the country were lifted by the sporting community, yet, the team found itself thoroughly outmatched on its first attempt to qualify for the finals - both continental and international. South Africa would win just one of their six qualifying games for the 1994 AFCON and fail to make it past the early stages of the 1994 FIFA World Cup, CAF qualifiers.

In the knockouts on their debut, South Africa eliminated African footballing giants Algeria and Ghana in back-to-back rounds before beating Tunisia to the title back in Johannesburg in front of an even larger crowd.
